Executive Summary
DDSAnalytics was engaged by Frito Lay to identify drivers of employee attrition and build predictive models to support proactive retention. Using data from 870 employees, we identified key attrition factors, built two predictive models, and quantified the financial impact.
Key Findings
Analysis identified three primary attrition drivers: overtime, monthly income, and marital status. Supporting factors included age, job level, years of experience, manager tenure, and job role. Entry level employees account for 61.4% of all attrition, and Sales Representatives show the highest within role quit rate at 45.3%.
Model Performance
The optimized Naïve Bayes model with a lowered decision threshold achieved 68% sensitivity and 61% specificity. Optimized KNN model achieved 65% Sensitivity and 67% Specificity
Financial Impact
The optimized Naïve Bayes model cost $1.32 million
Recommendation
Deploy the Naïve Bayes model with a 30% decision threshold, with quarterly HR reviews of flagged employees. Priority focus should be on entry level, overtime, and lower income employees in Sales, Lab Technician, and Research Scientist roles.
